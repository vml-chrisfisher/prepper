Weekly Newsletter No. 30
Hi All,

Happy Wednesday as usual. As of late, I’ve had this garden concept, I’ve been talking about in my head and planning how to move forward with it. I think it’s the idea that it should look and produce like the Victory Garden I remember watching on PBS when I was little. Everything just seemed to always be groomed perfectly. Though while talking to anyone that would listen about it, a friend of mine asked if I had heard about worm towers. While I had at first, I have gone down the rabbit hole on this one, and its composting that might fit my desire to do so and the need for my wife that it’s all done behind the scenes. And with that let’s get to it.
Asparagus, English Peas, and Capers Salad
Cooking
Traders Joe’s is about the only place I know that consistently carries English Peas in the Spring. I’ve tweaked this Asparagus, English Peas, and Capers Salad recipe throughout the years, looking to find some that provided the salty balance this salad needed that was too heavy.
This Is What Happens When There Are Too Many Meetings by The Atlantic
Reading
An interesting read about how we as a society have slowly developed a third wave of productivity since the pandemic began. Though not necessarily good, This Is What Happens When There Are Too Many Meetings shines a light on how flexibility has not just shifted how and when we work but also how the 250% increase in meetings has forced this third wave.
We Are The by Jon Batiste
Listening
If you have never heard of Jon Batiste or hadn’t heard about him until he swept the Grammy’s the other night, We Are The is the album to introduce yourself to him. Freedom will just make your soul want more and your body will want to jive. Watch the video for it and you will want to learn to as well.
The Felice Brothers
Listening
I’m not sure how I stumbled upon The Felice Brothers though when I first heard them, I swore I was listening to a Dylon side project where the depth of instruments was just deeper than normal. Their self-titled album from 2018 is just amazing from front to back and their latest From Dream to Dust doesn’t disappoint as well.

That’ll do it folks. Be good to each other.
