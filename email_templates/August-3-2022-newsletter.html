Weekly Newsletter No. 43
Hi all,

Happy Wednesday, as always. Believe it or not, this newsletter is the first one of August. This means that the fall season is getting even closer. While I’m not a fan of shorter days, I am also not a fan of weather that is inescapably hot. Plus I have to admit, I can’t wait to be wearing flannels, jackets, and all that good stuff. I feel more at home in those clothes than I do in shorts if that makes sense?

I was able to build the raised garden beds this last weekend. For a person that doesn’t build things on the regular, I”m pretty proud of these. There will always be better ways I could have done certain things but for my first go, I’ll take them. With this extreme heat and my excitement to have things growing in these newly minted raised beds, my excitement for next year has already come. This long thinking tends to have my mind go fast ahead, forgetting the laundry list of tasks that need to happen before Spring. Mainly getting the compost going inside of the beds and then the larger worm tower. I had a grand plan for it, though I think for the time simple may be the correct route.
Curing Bacon
Cooking
Summer is obviously tomato season, which screams trying to perfect your BLT. Though in my family, I’m the only one who likes, tomatoes, lettuce, or mayo. So, I have a BLT, while the rest of them make a sandwich that includes bacon, mostly. Though I always find that making your own bacon, is not only simple but makes for a better BLT. Curing of any type tends to scare people away but bacon is the simplest cure you can find and pretty impossible to find. Once you make your own, store-bought will just never be the same.
Obviously by Lake Street Dive
Listening
Lake Street Dive was a band I found listening to a random mix from either Apple Music or Spotify. The Boston-based band is marked as an alternative band though they always feel more 70’s. I’ve always been a fan of their Free Yourself Up album along with their latest Obviously. They are releasing a new album in September and I'll be more than happy to show the vocals of Bridget Kearney to my daughter. She will always make Kate want to grab a wooden spoon and mouth the lyrics with the best of them. If it comes from through as a strong woman, I’ll always encourage that.
Root Cellars: Types and Storage Tips by Sandy Newton
Reading
We have a space below our basement stairs that makes one want to focus it to a root cellar space. The aging shelves need to be removed and the space clean in general. Though reading Root Cellar by Sandy Newton gives a quick read on moving forward on this idea along with note of adding to our canning storage.
Build a Bee House for Solitary Bees by Stephanie Rose
Reading
I’ve recently started concentrating on having enough pollinator flowers in or around the garden to help increase crops. Though I never knew much about bees more than the honey or bumble bees. Though, as always, reading gave me the knowledge to know that the vast majority of bees are actually solitary, only meeting to mate and then separating. Just like the social bees, solitary bees need help creating nesting. Build a Bee House for Solitary Bees lays out all of the information and how to create a bee house for the solitary variety. Something that has been added to our list. We saw an increase in bees this year and always welcome more.


That’s it for this week. Enjoy the last of the heat of summer, in 6 months we will all be begging for it. Grass is always greener.
