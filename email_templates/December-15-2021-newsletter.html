Weekly Newsletter No. 16
Hi all,

Hope this middle of the week seems little more on the downhill slide as everyone starts to wind down for the year. This year has been an interesting one for me. One that started with me losing a lot of who I was. Taking the second half to dig deep in ways I never knew were there. As with my cooking I was able to rebuild my foundation. Letting go of blockers. Refocusing my thought process. I’ve done that with my cooking. Focusing on what it means to be Creole. How can Black cooking and French cooking be moved forward even further? I’ve had a rolling list of recipes in my head of recipes I wanted to make this year. My first stab at trying to find balance in this mixture of cultures and cooking. While I am proud of what I’ve produced, I feel like a lack of technique is coming to the surface. It’s lead me, to want to work harder on my cooking techniques. Knife skills. Timing. Taste balance. Basics. Those are the main 4 that I’m after. It’s more about refinement than anything. And with that, let’s get to it.
Smoked Trout Dip
Cooking
Looking back, I’ve never had a bad meal with smoked fish. This Smoke Rainbow Trout Dip requires a day of brining and cold smoke. With that in mind, I would smoke more fish than you need. Add it to a risotto.
Benjamin Booker
Listening
If you’ve never heard of Benjamin Booker you are in for an amazing surprise. Blue, punk and raw. His first self-titled album will take you back to when you first heard the White Stripes. Violent Shiver is simply amazing. Unfiltered and punk like quality you can’t quite put your finger on but just enjoy. His second album Witness is more emotional. Believe pulls on you like it wants to be performed with a full choir. The Slow Drag Under takes you to an old jive joint and wanting to perform some type of dance that only your soul will take you.
Cell Alpha
Musing
There is lot of news going on these days. It makes it hard to find something beyond the noise. Though Cell Alpha is an interesting concept in the audio space. Developed by an Apple engineer who left and who has now come back to further development on the Home Pod. It will interesting to see where this goes even if the price is on the ridiculous.

That’s it, folks. Be good and make sure to hang those stockings.
