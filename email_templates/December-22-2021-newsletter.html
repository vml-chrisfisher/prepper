Weekly Newsletter No. 17
Hi all,

Hoping this Wednesday finds you as excited as I am for this holiday week. I’d be foolish to think that this Christmas will be the same as years past. Though I am encouraged by the progress, a little or a lot, by whose definition you go by, that this holiday people are starting to celebrate a little farther out of their circle. Christmas always feels like the holiday where traditions are held firm and the evolution of new ones is slow. There is a torch that gets handed over. A sense of responsibility of blending two families' traditions of the holiday. It’s fascinating to think how different traditions have been blended over the lifetime of Christmas. Different cultures, with their version, mix in with families who decided to splice together the tradition of two different religions. It shows how closely woven we really are. A little deep but hey sometimes that’s what happens when you try to not go there on Christmas. With that, let’s get to it.
Shrimp & Grits
Cooking
We have traditionally always had Shrimp & Grits on Christmas morning. After the gifts, we usually sit down for what turns into brunch. I tend to like my Shrimp & Grits to have a mellow buttery sauce to let the shrimp shine.
Pecan Pralines
Cooking
I’ve made many Christmas goodies, cookies, and dessert tarts. Though it’s been a while since I’ve dived into the candy section. This year I decided to dust off the Pecan Pralines recipes and also give Salted Caramels a try as well. These Pecan Pralines are nothing fancy, just good.
Miyabi Artisan Paring Knife
Musing
Your paring knife should feel like a good pocket knife. Something you can easily peel a piece of rind for some lemon zest or to peel an apple. The thinnest of a Japanese blade seems to lend more toward this. I’ve had my eye on this Miyabi Artisan Paring Knife for a while though have held off due to the Christmas moratorium for personal purchases.

That’s a wrap for this week. Enjoy the lights before they are gone.
