Weekly Newsletter No. 15
Hello all,

Hope all is well. This last weekend was a busy one but one I’ve been counting down for a while. Saturday we had some of our closest friends. It had been three to four years to many that we had hosted a dinner party and for us, it was sadly missed. My cooking has shifted into a journey of Black and French cooking and techniques in search of what truly is Creole, beyond the classics. It was good to share where I am on that path. There is this drive in me that it is always pursuing this perfection in recipes and techniques. Finding balance in acid. Working on prep far enough in advance to keep the knife skills there. As if there is an ongoing seasonal menu that I am working to perfect. Maybe these dinner parties are my answer to a dream of a restaurant or maybe the precursor. I’m sure the first and not that latter. Either way, it keeps me striving and always hungry for knowledge of what it means to be that narrowness of Creole. That’s it, folks, keep hungry and let's get to it.

Charles Wesley Godwin
Listening
Charles Wesley Godwin. Since the release of his first album Seneca in 2019, I have been yearning for more releases from this artist. He has a way of bending Appalachian string music with a bit of rocking guitar in a way that simply works. The old-school Americana feel that he produces is second to none, and his new album How the Mighty Fall was one of my most awaited releases of this year. It was released a few weeks back and I have found myself putting this album on shuffle just about every time I get in the car.

That’s it, folks. Be good and keep warm.
