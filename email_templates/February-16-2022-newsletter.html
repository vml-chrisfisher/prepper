Weekly Newsletter No. 25
Happy Wednesday Everyone,

This tends to be the time of year when I start to feel like I’m in a meal purgatory - wanting to release the heaviness of winter meals and bring in the freshness of spring. To me, this is when Mother Nature starts to fully release the last year. Buds are beginning to bloom, outside laughs from the neighborhood kids are becoming more frequent. Not sure about you, but there seems to be a release in the air. With the Super Bowl over and the temperatures starting to flirt north, I’m not sure if you would call it a release of relief or joy. Either way, it has me feeling hopeful and walking with a little extra hip in the step. These could all be naive thoughts, but I tend to lean into those, and with this one, I’m ok being the trusting one. Alright, let’s get to this.
The Last Word
Cooking
My brother-in-law recently introduced me to this tasty drink known as The Last Word. The Last Word was first served at the Detroit Athletic Club around 1915 and it hits the notes for moving into spring. Gin, green chartreuse, fresh lime juice, with a brandied cherry as a nod to all of the Manhattan’s from the last winter
The Untold Story of Super Bowl LVI Stadium in Los Angeles
Reading
SoFi stadium has always been an interesting venue for me. From its design restraints with LAX to the fact that SoFi was given naming rights to the stadium. Though I’m always a sucker for a good architecture read and The Untold Story of Super Bowl LVI Stadium in Los Angeles gives a quick insight about the stadium design and innovation, especially toward its landscaping and piece toward a larger mix-use space.
A Jacobson Finishing Flake Salt
Using
I think I first discovered flake salt when I made the Salted Carmel Chocolate Tart. It’s now become a standard workhorse in my kitchen. I’ve never ventured past Jacobson’s Flake Salt, but with their nod toward sustainable harvesting and push toward sustainable honey harvesting, it seems like staying put is the right foot forward at this moment.
SpaceX’s Polaris Mission Could Kickstart A New Era For Private Spaceflight
Reading
If you haven’t watched the Netflix Series about the Space X Inspiration4 mission, you absolutely should. You’ll get a glimpse of the vision that is pushing our space program’s envelope. “SpaceX’s Polaris Mission Could Kickstart A New Era For Private Spaceflight” reads about a new set of private missions to help further Musk’s vision of colonizing Mars. It’s fascinating to watch this all take fold.
St. Paul & The Broken Bones
Listening
Based out of Birmingham, St. Paul & The Broken Bones music hit’s with a 60’s/70’s soul gospel feel. Their lead singer, Paul Janeway’s, vocals just flow with a jive that mixes well with the complex horn arrangements. Take a walk while enjoying their album Sea of Noise, and it will pick up your pace and leave you feeling optimistic. Their Alien Coast album venture removes the gospel feel, but is something that is always welcomed on a Sunday morning.

That’ll do it folks.
