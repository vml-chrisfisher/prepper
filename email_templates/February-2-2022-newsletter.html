Weekly Newsletter No. 23
Happy Wednesday,

We’ve entered February, and as always, January set a quick pace and we are moving through another year at a pretty good clip. February is an odd month for me. After the holidays, winter can be a trudge, so my soul gets antsy for spring. In this neck of the woods, Mother Nature doesn’t help. Giving us tiny spurts of spring-like weather, to then only slam us right back to brutal cold temps. I always find many people get caught up in the feeling of losing too much time after January has passed. I tend to try and ride the wave of this month with the anticipation of spring, but with little expectations of much else. A quick look at any current revisions I have going on and a glance to make sure none have slipped. With that, keep moving forward, and let’s get to it.
Shrimp and Okra Perloo
Cooking
When you describe a Perloo to someone, jambalaya typically comes to mind. While a cousin, a Perloo is meant to be simple. Often made with okra and some type of cubed pork, like bacon or tasso ham, and then of course Carolina Golden Rice. Almost every culture has some version of a Perloo, which is why there is often a spelling difference, the most famous being Pilaf. Try this Shrimp and Okra Perloo, even if you are not an okra lover.
Why Do People Put Peanuts in Coke?
Reading
I’m always surprised when I found out people have never heard of putting a sleeve of peanuts in a Coke, much less even tried it. “Why Do People Put Peanuts in Coke?”, is an interesting read on the theory about how it all got started. I’ve also heard of using an orange soda. While both are tasty, I tend to go back to Coke. I'm a sucker for articles like this - small little habits or traditions and how they got started. It helps me maintain my trove of useless facts.
Vignettes by Wesley Schultz
Listening
If you don’t recognize the name Wesley Schultz, you’ll surely recognize his voice as the frontman for the Lumineers. ‘Vignettes’ is Wesley's first album as a solo artist and it doesn’t disappoint. All of the songs in the album are covers and the whole thing feels like an MTV Unplugged session. All of the songs are redone with such restraint. Stripping back each song down to only what was needed and then slowly adding back simplistic elements. The choir-like background singers give the album a Southern church feel, which feels right, because Wesley definitely takes some of these songs to the altar.
HAIM Tiny Desk NPR Tiny Desk Session
Listening
Seeing Alina Haim all over the place for her new movie Licorice Pizza reminded me of how good the band's NPR Tiny Desk session was. Watching it now brings me back to what feels like a different world. For reference, it was only three months after March of 2020. Vaccines had yet to come out, we had a different president, it wasn’t clear whether every restaurant and movie theater would close or not. Listening to this session is another example of listening to an artist strip down their sound. This one due to the separation the pandemic caused.

Alright folks, that’ll do it. Have a great weekend, stay warm, and take care.
