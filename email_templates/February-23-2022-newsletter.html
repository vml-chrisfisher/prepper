Weekly Newsletter No. 26
Hey All,

Happy Wednesday as always. The midwestern weather roller coaster has been epic this year. In the 70’s one day and then a flip to a major snowstorm the next. Last weekend warmed up nicely. Now granted, it was too cold to go out without a jacket, but standing in the sun just hit me differently. Birds are starting to come out, along with the return of our neighborhood owls every once in a while. In the hopes of spring being around the corner, it’s easy to be in good spirits. And on that note, let’s get to it.
Sazerac
Cooking
People like to say the Sazerac is a close cousin to an Old Fashioned. To me, it is more of New Orleans’ “hold my beer” response to it. The history, and even the true ingredients in a Sazerac, falls in line with a lot of food that stems from New Orleans culture. I prefer using rye whiskey instead of brandy.
The Fall and Rise of Florida Oysters
Reading
The third article about oysters that I’ve shared, “The Fall and Rise of Florida Oysters,” discusses how Florida is another coastal region fighting to bring back the oyster industry. These reads peak my interest regarding the commonality every coastline shares, but each one offers something different to the palate.
The California Honeydrops
Listening
The California Honeydrops are a blues band that makes you want to dance in place. Their Call It Home album seems to hit the spot right now. I think it’s the good mix of horns that make me want to march winter away and welcome spring. Either way, give them a listen. You won’t be disappointed.

That’ll do it folks. Be good to each other.

Cheers,
Chris
