Weekly Newsletter No. 24
Hi All,

Happy Wednesday as always. I hope you’re having a good week. I’m not sure what’s in the air or the water, but this has been a week of nostalgia for me. When I think about it, there’s a desire to establish a place to make memories for the kids and us. I’ve always thought that family vacation homes show memories like an old pair of jeans. Denim wears in and changes so drastically from its original state. What’s funny is that the changes are not even perceptible. You wear your jeans, then one day you pull them out of the wash and you see patterns of wear. It’s a little magical, but also just how time works. Wearing in memories slowly, ya know? And with that, let’s get to it.
Tasso Ham
Cooking
Tasso Ham is a specialty, that according to the history books, comes from southern Louisiana. Made from pork shoulder, I tend to lean more on this as the South’s pancetta. Usually diced, salty, smokey, and earthy from its curing rub.
France, Oysters and a Playlist
Musing
We were asked to join some friends on the West coast of France for a summer vacation. La Flotte is known for its oysters. Grilled oysters, mignonette, hot sauce, yellow beers, champagne, and playlist based on Me and You by Jamie Lidell seem like a path worth going down. No matter how we go, I’m excited.
A Chef Grapples With Threats to a Seafood Tradition
Reading
It seems like the coasts are driving the movement to address changes from Mother Nature, which usually happens when economics are threatened A Chef Grabbles with Threat to a Seafood Traditions writes about the fight Melissa Martin is leading to help save the fishermen and their coasts. It helps to show how our decisions of the past are snowballing at an alarming rate, that we can’t keep up to save what is uniquely ours as a country. It also enforces the need for us, as humans, to try harder and faster. Thanks, Emily, for letting me know about this one.
Yaniv
Playing
When you boil down the articles on this card game, Israeli soldiers, fast-paced, and cheating seem to be the common theme. Introduced to it this past weekend Yaniv is now on the vacation travel list.
Piet Oudolf’s Gardens Stars and Flowers
Reading
Our flower beds and the plants we inherited scream to be flowing beds of seasonal color. Reading about Piet Oudolf’s garden will encourage you to embrace his thinking about the ecosystem and the balance in creating what nature would have wanted there. His thoughts about a garden being interactive are fascinating.
Listening
After asking my sister-in-law the name of a band playing, my brother-in-law asked if I was caught up with Swamp Dogg. Which caused me to go down a path that lead me back to Durand James and the Indications. A sound that always leans heavily on the R&B of the ’60s and ’70s this band is a bit more polished with every album. Their latest album Private Space is another evolution of this.

Alright folks, that’ll do it. Have a great weekend, hopefully you will be able to enjoy the up and down temperatures we have here. As always take care.

Cheers,
Chris
