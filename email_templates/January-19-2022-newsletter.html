Weekly Newsletter No. 21
Hello all,

Happy Wednesday to all! I would love to report that the family and I have come back from Minnesota refreshed and possibly have a shot from a frozen lake. Unfortunately, after avoiding it for two years, the kids and my wife finally made us a sick house. To say these last seven days have been brutal would be an understatement. To abruptly lose your partner with the day-to-day and then to tend to the needs of everyone can make one feel helpless. It makes it hard to find a little bit of breath and happiness in the middle of it all. It was nice to see the snow come in and allow the kids to go out and just be free for a bit. I feel this snow meant a little bit more to them and based on the smiles at all of the neighborhood hills, I think it meant the same for a lot of kids. This winter seems overly tough and the eggheads show that now is the time of year when New Year’s resolutions start to slip and most of us won't see them through. Give yourself some grace this year. Keep pressing upwards and onwards and hopefully check things off the To-Do list. With that let’s get going.
Hot Chocolate
Cooking
Simple things made really good seem necessary these days. With Winter setting in heavy, an amazing Hot Chocolate seems like a childhood simplicity that is fitting right now. This recipe is an older one but always hits the mark of rich and the technique will bring back memories of how your grandmother would have made it.
Repairing Sweaters
Reading
It’s all too easy to replace something the second it has a hole in it. The other day, I was doing a purge of my closet and as I threw out worn our clothes the first thing I did was look up replacements, but that's not really the right attitude to have if you’re working to be more sustainable. As I was thinking about this, Put This On published an article about repairing sweaters, which must be a sign. I’m excited to breathe some new life into my old clothes. 
SeaVees Mammoth Boot
Musing
With a new puppy in the house, potty training bring a lot of in and out in the snow. The tying and untying of shoes is just another in the moment exhausting task. These boots from SeaVees Mammoth Boots seemed to fit the mark until I realized they were tie as well. I’m hoping there is a solution for this and will soon being pulling the trigger on these.
Nathaniel Rateliff & The Night Sweats
Listening
I’ve told you about Nathaniel Rateliff in the past and it seems like a punt on my part if I didn’t tell you about his music with the Night Sweats. The Night Sweats seems to make his music richer. The kids have made My Mama their bedtime song repeatedly these last couple of week and I’m not sad about it.
Alright folks that will do it. Keep warm and stay vigilante on your mental health.
