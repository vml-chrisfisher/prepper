Weekly Newsletter No. 22
Hi all,

Another Happy Wednesday as always. After these last ten days, it’s safe to say we have a clean house once again and the relief of weight that comes with that is needed. The Chiefs' victory over the Bills was a great sense of relief as well though the emotions of this city were felt with every second of the game of those final 13. This past week, I felt lethargic and spacey. I’m sure it comes with the exhaustion from the last couple of weeks. We tend to run hard in this house, so I’m trying to lean into what my body is telling me. Instead of attempting to muscle through it and force me to push harder, I’m trying to accept how I feel and give myself time to rest. Whether resting means sleeping, reading, or simply giving myself more time to think about things. It provided me the direction to start sharpening my knives and that felt right. With that let’s get to it.
Crispy Salt and Pepper Chicken Wings
Cooking
A friend of mine brought over some Buffalo wings and while delicious, it just reminded me how much I hate the mess. If you fall into my line of thinking, try these Crispy Salt and Pepper Chicken Wings. The baking soda will feel weird but it’s what dries the skin to get a good crispy wing.
Japan’s Shoeshine Champions Explain Proper Shoe Care
Reading
Obviously, these days there haven’t been many reasons to get dressed up which has made all of my dress shoes a bit dusty and in need of some care. It feels off to find interest in a shoeshine competition but also exciting the amount of bespoke knowledge you can gain from it. Japan’s Shoeshine Champions Explain Proper Shoe Care is a start down this path. Even if you’re not the type to shine your own shoes, there are some good tidbits to help maintain them. I’m still curious to figure out what “movement” is in a shoeshine.
Olive Oil
Musing
Removing a lasagna from the oven, I recently knocked over my olive oil dispenser and it shatter all over the container. It had been on the list of things to replace and that incident just brought it to the top. These ceramic ones from Etsy seem like the right choice since they won’t let in light. But also don’t quite hit the mark for me. The search is on, so send me any suggestions.
Mondo Cozmo
Listening
Tunes that were less bourbon and a bit more releasing were needed after this last week. Mondo Cozmo’s Plastic Soul album definitely fits that bill. It feels like an album that if Bono had a sound machine this is what he would have produced. Though Mondo Cozmo is less political in nature. Give it a listen and you will understand when I say every song feels like a release.

Alright, that will do it this week. Stay safe and take care of each other out there.

Cheers,
Chris
