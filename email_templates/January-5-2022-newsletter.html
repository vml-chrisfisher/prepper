Weekly Newsletter No. 19
Hello all,

First newsletter of the year and I hope this finds you well. For many, this marks the time to start new things, and while I can appreciate that thinking, I tend to lean towards this being a touchpoint on a revision. I’m sure the programmer in me helps drive this thinking - never finished, just revising. Though it also helps me stay grounded. It never feels right to set lofty goals that seem unachievable in the first place. Small revisions always suit me better, with this newsletter being one of them. Looking at the numbers, it seems fitting to shift some people over to a monthly newsletter and keep the others right here. It’s been a joy writing this newsletter and revising this little corner of the internet. With that, let's get to it.
Blackcreek Black Food - Stories, Art, and Recipes from Across the African Diaspora
Reading
A long holding tradition, my sister-in-law has always given me a cookbook for my birthday and Xmas. Black Food is more than a cookbook, Bryant Terry explores Black culture through food, poems, essays and notes of his experiences.
Notes from a Young Black Chef: A Memoir
Reading
A deep dive into the rise, fall and everything in between, about the rising chef, Kwame Onwuachi, winner of the 2019 James Beard Foundation Award for Rising Star Chef of the Year. I’ve always been a of Sean Brock and his story. This memoir seems like the beginning of another chef fascination.
Black Girl Baking
Reading
I first learned about Jerrelle Guy while watching High on the Hog. Her spirit and passion for food is amazing. Her ability to take her memories and translate them through food is inspiring to say the least. All of this energy comes through the pages of Black Girl Baking.
That’s it, good luck leaning into your revisions.

Cheers,
Chris
