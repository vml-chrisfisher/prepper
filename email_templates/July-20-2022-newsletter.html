Hello All,

It’s another Wednesday. Cooking hasn’t been top of mind, but the space for next year's garden has been. On that note, let's get to it.
@thegirlyblackfarmer
Following
The story of Black farmers in America has always interested me. Historically, farmers were predominately Black. But as racism crept its way in, the Black farmer became another minority within our country. It’s heartening to see that Black farming is making a resurgence, and following the journey of the @thegirlyblackfarmer on Instagram is inspiring.

Reading
Finding articles from the Rodale Institute about Rengerative Agriculture has added a new spark to the ecosystem of my cooking. Having more knowledge about the food I cook has become fascinating to me. Reading How to Practice Regenerative Agriculture in Your Own Home Garden by Erin Bunch has given me a lot of inspiration to launch my own little garden.
Sheet Mulching
Reading
After researching a good amount, it seems like sheet mulching is a great way to convert the ground from lawn to rich gardening soil. Oregon State University has created a great step-by-step article on how to get this going. Also known as lasagna composting, it simply layers organic materials - one being rich in carbon and the other rich in nitrogen. Simple and proven, which is a path I’ll always take.
Growing Perennial Food: A Field Guide to Raising Resilient Herbs Fruits and Vegetables by Acadia Tucker
Reading
Acadia Tucker is one of the experts on Regenerative Gardens and has written several books on the subject. Creating a garden that thrives throughout the array of Missouri seasons is an interesting idea that I think is worth pursuing. Growing Perennial Food: A Field Guide to Raising Resilient Herbs Fruits and Vegetables by Acadia Tucker is a stepping stone to getting there, and I look forward to gaining knowledge.
