Weekly Newsletter No. 42
Happy Wednesday,

Last Sunday, the clouds rolled in with a mild rain shower and a drop of 20 degrees. I happened to be outside when the shift occurred and it was well received. These lower temps are predicted to stay for a bit, which is great as I shift from planning to constructing a garden. In addition to 3 or 4 beds, I want to convert my 5-gallon bucket worm tower into a larger scale. If everything calculates correctly, I should be able to convert all of my yearly grass clippings and leaves into rich compost. Composting has always interested me, but the slow compost has always confused me, because they seem too difficult to actually get meaningful compost. Worm towers give you a visual of where things came from and where they are going. I’m sure this comes from my forever need for a feedback loop.

On that note, let's get to it.
Tomato and Olive Salad with Burrata
Cooking
If there is a food that defines summer it is the tomato. The memory of biting into a freshly grown tomato with a bit of salt is one I love going back to every summer. This Tomato and Olive Salad with Burrata gets its salt and umami flavor from the olives and capers.

Let the tomatoes, olives, and capers meld their flavors together before serving. I like to pull the salad out of the refrigerator a couple of hours before serving. Also, a nice crack of fresh ground pepper on top of the burrata cheese is always delicious.
Durolux T5 Ho Grow Light
Buying
I took a second read of Growing Perennial Food: A Field Guide to Raising Resilient Herbs Fruits and Vegetables by Acadia Tucker and now that my list of fruits and veggies has grown, I’ve partly started looking at growing seedlings. Unfortunately, our south-facing windows are not conducive for growing seedlings, so I’ve been researching grow lights for the basement. The Durolux T5 Ho Grow Light seems to be a crowd favorite, but I’m sure a visit to a local store will help immensely.
Jiffy Peat Pots
Buying
Peat pots are needed with seedlings, and ordering via Amazon seems like a smart choice. I’ve never understood the plastic pots that usually get dumped in the recycling every year. I’ve used Jiffy Peat Pots in the past, and while the science tends to lean towards them not being as eco-friendly as we thought, it is still a step forward until I can find a better solution.


That’s it for this week. Enjoy the cooling weather if you're here in Kansas City.
