Happy Thursday everyone. Sending this on Thursday makes me feel at odds with myself. This newsletter began as a way for me to take on Knife and Fish in my own stride. So for me to miss not only a week but then to miss the second by a day, feels like a loss, in my book. I apologize for the hiccup but the flu came to me and then throw in the holiday weekend and things happened. Things are moving back to normal and it’s that thought that I’ve decided to concentrate on. Several of you have reached out about the progressive dinner and I couldn’t have been prouder of what I set out to create. I had some hits and some misses but in my mind, it’s progression forward that I’m happy with. Though to present something that feels like me and my exploration of the balance of French and Black cooking and its melding that created Creole just felt good. The Crispy Chicken Skins turned out amazing and I’ve added the recipe below. Beyond that, we are leaning full forward toward our trip to France. French cooking and culture pull at my soul just as much as Black cooking, so this trip feels needed. With that, let’s get going.
Crispy Chicken Skins with Hot Honey, Creme Fraiche, and a Caviar Bump
Cooking
There’s an old French recipe that takes potato chips and serves them with Creme Fraiche and Caviar. This spin takes that elegance and adds so much more but not too much. The Crispy Chicken Skins become the perfect vessel to serve this along with the balance of Hot Honey and Creme Fraiche. The Caviar bump just adds that much more.
Brightside Bonus Tracks by the Lumineers
Listening
I’m a sucker for artists taking a selection of songs and spinning them into an acoustic version. The Lumineers added to the collection with Brightside Bonus Tracks. While they have redone a couple of their more favorited tracks they decided to do a cover of The Cure’s Just Like Heaven. It truly just pulls you in wanting just that much more.

Alright, that will do it this time.
