Hi All,

Happy Wednesday as always. It seems like Mother Nature has been pretty emotional these days, sending our temperatures into a beautiful 75 degrees one day and then slamming us down with hailing snow the next. This behavior certainly encourages me to appreciate those mid 70’s and to take advantage of them. A lot of planning occurred in our house during this last week of great weather. We have several vacations in the works, with spring break right around the corner. But I think the progressive dinner we are hosting with three other couples is what has me most excited. I have a base direction for four of the courses and will probably end up with six or seven by the time I add some smaller items. Lately, I’ve been keeping a notebook of recipe ideas, which has helped me focus and refine the menu. With that, let’s get to it.
Littleneck Clams in White Wine Sauce
Cooking
Mussels in White Wine is a classic recipe with mussels steamed in garlicky white wine sauce. It’s always a welcomed treat, but I tend to prefer Littleneck clams over mussels. This Littleneck Clams in White Wine recipe begs for you to grab a chunk of baguette with some butter and then dip it in the sauce to soak it up.
Sharpening Knives from The Manual
Reading
I’ve been trying to take better care of kitchen items, and sharpening my own knives has been something I’ve been working on. Best Way to Sharpen Knives from The Manual gives a pretty good breakdown on all of the ways to sharpen knives. To me, a whetstone is the only way. The concept is not necessarily a hard one, but it’s the technique. I’m working toward getting that perfect 15 to 17-degree angle. While I’m a ways off from becoming a blade smith, my cutting skills have benefitted from this as well.
Gardens by The Heavy Hours
Listening
The Heavy Hours came up as a recommendation after listening to Mondo Conzmo for so long. Gardens is their sophomore album and reminds me of Jakob Dylan and his band the Wallflowers. I know at first you will think that statement is off but give them a minute and maybe you will align with me on it. As someone from Kansas City, I’m not too keen on recommending anything from Cincinnati, but these four guys have something.

That’ll do it folks. Be good to each other.
