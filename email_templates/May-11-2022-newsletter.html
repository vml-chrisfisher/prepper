Weekly Newsletter No. 35
Hi All,

It’s halfway through the week, and even though the temps have shot up to summer levels, the excessive amount of green this year’s April showers produced has to have everyone in a good mood. We took a moment on Mother’s Day to add some color to our flower beds, and with mulch coming this week, things are starting to come together outdoors. We are in an all-on sprint to get ready for the progressive dinner that I’ve mentioned. You never really realize how behind you are on the list of honey-dos until they need to be done all at once. While I feel like I’m making fairly good progress on the list, I seem to add more tasks than I can cross off. In the end, it will end up being a great time outside, with old and new friends, good food and drink. With that, let's get going.
Pâte Brisée
Cooking
Having a great pâte brisée crust on hand seems like a good idea, so it’s always within reach for a quiche or a tart. Though lately, galettes, the rustic version of a tart, are where I tend to lean. However, I tend to add more savory fillings than sweet. Either way, they all need an amazing crust and a pâte brisée is the only way to go.
Miles Benjamin Anthony Robinson
Listening
Sometimes the recommendations from Spotify or Apple Music seem to come out of nowhere. And while I thought that would be the case for Miles Benjamin Anthony Robinson I was so off. This guy feels Bon Iver, but pulls guitar like Lenny Kravitz. Only two albums so far and I’m really hoping something new is on its way.

That's a rap around here. Enjoy the weather.
