Weekly Newsletter No. 36
Happy Wednesday as always,

If there’s ever a moment these weekly thoughts feel random, it’s primarily because I am always adding to a random list of thoughts and links throughout the week. However, it felt good to consistently do it outside while enjoying decent temperature this last week. With our progressive dinner happening this weekend, there hasn’t been time to create a new recipe or dig one out of the archives. So this week is truly a collection of articles and music that have caught my interest. This whole website started with a wide net of thought on food and then I felt it needed to be focused. However, it has morphed, especially with the weekly list of randomness, and I tend to like it. It allows me to release some energy. With that, let’s get to it.
Segregation By Design: How one architect is visualizing the legacy of America’s racist urbanism
Reading
A great read on a project trying to focus on visualizing the redlining in America and how it shifted cities. It explores how segregation methods from supposed neutral parties have affected cities today. It’s based on the book The Color of Law, which I’m ready to dig into as well.
Thailand home connects to its beautiful surroundings
Reading
Reading past issues, it’s not hard to see that architecture blending with nature is something I am willing to share. “Thailand home connects to its beautiful surroundings” is a Thai home that sits on a former rice paddy. The house is simple and thoughtful in its intention.
11 perfect tiny plants to decorate your tiny home
Reading
My daughter recently created a small succulent garden with my mother in law. We have many house plants, but it inspired me to think about other small plants we could have throughout the house. “11 perfect tiny plants to decorate your tiny home” might encourage me to try Kokedama or even the more traditional known version of bonsai.
Fade Into You by Valerie June
Listening
Finding out certain musicians do a specific cover is exciting. Valerie June’s release of Fade Into You is just that. Her voice carries so well with the melodic flow of the song.

Alright, that will do it this time.
