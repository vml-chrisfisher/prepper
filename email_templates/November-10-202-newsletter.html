Weekly Newsletter No. 11
Happy Wednesday to all,

The kids were invited to a last-minute sleepover on Saturday, which teed the wife and me up for an impromptu date night. It felt good to enjoy some drinks at Ca Va and end at Corvino’s. We have always said a good night ends at Corvino’s with a hamburger and fries. Even after 18 plus months of being away it still doesn’t disappoint. While Ca Va is always perfect to discover a new wine, Corvino’s bar is a well-kept secret that I always like to enjoy. Name a liquor and just let the dealer serve his/her choice. Add on a day full of the kids playing with their neighborhood friends and it makes you feel like things are moving in a “normal” direction. And with that, let’s go to it.
Reading
Having several irons in the fire to move your income forward has always seemed like a right-minded goal. To read How Ryan Reynolds Built a Business Empire is an inspiration, even if the scale is different.
Listening
Not sure if nostalgia is in play, but this week has me listening to more R&B. My Dad wanting to be known for having a library of great classic R&B comes to mind. Sifting through lists made from the likes of Otis Redding, Wilson Pickett, Al Green and Marvin Gaye, landed me on Charles Bradley. It’s inspiring to learn about someone finding their fame later in life. Though when you hear him, you won’t be wrong to think his albums were from the past. Listening to him will remind you about the R&B that is known as the “good stuff.”
Cooking
Thanksgiving is coming in like a freight train. While it’s one of my favorite holidays, there is always a bit of pressure. I’ve been placed in charge of several dishes, with the ham being one. I’ve always preferred purchasing a fresh ham and curing it myself. I’m sure I will tweak the brining recipe for a fresh ham I have on the Knife and Fish site because it’s always a good base. Though I’ll be sure to post any updates.
Musing
If you’ve known me a long while or even a short, I’m sure I’ve told you of memories about my grandfather and his career with boats. It has always given me an appreciation for wooden boats, specifically the runabout type. While my grandfather’s dedication was to the Chris Craft brand, over the years Hackerboats have caught my eye with a family-style triple cockpit always at the top of the list. There’s something about a runabout that creates thoughts of having a place on a quiet piece of a lake with a boathouse, all there to create memories with the family. All a dream that my wife knows well too much about, that I’ve never been able to convince her to go after.
All right that’s it this week. Be well and take care of each other out there.

Cheers,
Chris
