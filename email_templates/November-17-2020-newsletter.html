Weekly Newsletter No. 12
Happy Wednesday,

It’s a little strange thinking about what's ahead. I can feel something on the horizon as we approach the rush at the end of the year. The calm before the storm, in other words. In a lot of ways, I want to get on with it, though apprehension surfaces based on recent history. Last weekend I was invited to leave the urban bubble and go to my first deer camp. The trip made me realize a few things: everyone is looking for normal and no one really knows what that originally was; a weekend away from the kids is always refreshing; most of us appreciate the slight bend of the middle that’s held us together. Those thoughts give me hope that this holiday season is shaping up to be something special. Our Thanksgiving menu is being finalized. All that’s left to do now is enjoy some downtime. Let’s get to it.
Beef Short Rib Pepper Pot
Cooking
Pepper Pots have always been a favorite soup of mine that many don’t know about. Traditionally, when people think of the American slave trade, they forget about the trail that leads to the Caribbean and the pepper pot. It’s easy to connect the dots between a pepper pot and gumbo. This Beef Short Rib Pepper Pot is buttery and perfect on top of some Mascarpone cheese grits. Scotch Bonnet peppers are traditionally added for spice. I lean towards adding a spiced okra relish.

Stu Larsen's
Marigold
Listening
I wouldn’t be surprised if you haven’t heard of Stu Larsen - a singer from Australia that’s lived as a nomad and only released a few albums. His latest album, Marigold, listens like a release of feelings from a breakup and just wants to tug at you.
All right, that’s it this week. Be well and make sure your people are, too.
