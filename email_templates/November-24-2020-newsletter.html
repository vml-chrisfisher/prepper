Weekly Newsletter No. 13
Happy Thanksgiving Eve,

It’s the holidays which for me also means my birthday. While I enjoy my birthday it’s Thanksgiving and the beginning of the holiday season is what does it for me. Normally this week is filled with a rush of prep and to-do lists, though this year there seems to be a calm to it all. Maybe it’s the feeling that we, as a human race, are starting to push forward, finding grace where it’s needed and healing a bit. Some may call me on being naive on this but for now, I’m ok with that. With that, let’s get to it.
Salted Caramel Chocolate Tart
Cooking
It’s the Wednesday before Thanksgiving and I know it’s not the time to throw something out there to make you call an audible on the menu. Though I’ll put this Salted Caramel Chocolate Tart out there for anyone needing a last-minute dessert.
The Regnerative Agriculture and the Soil Carbon Solution
Reading
You may have noticed that a lot of my reading has to do with the environment and how people are starting to think of solutions in scale. This whitepaper on Regenerative Farming from the Rodale Institute is fascinating. It’s hard to argue with science-based thinking on how to create a healthy circular system that helps repair the damage we’ve done to our planet.
Nathaniel Rateliff's
And It’s Still Alright
Listening
I encourage everyone to take a moment and find their space this weekend. It’s healthy to find space for yourself. I am always nostalgic and think of a little vinyl playing in the background. If you find your way to music Nathaniel Rateliff is amazing. While his latest album The Future is outstanding his last one And It’s Still Alright fits this moment even better.
That’s it, folks. Reach out to someone that has become old to you these last 2 years. Be good.

Cheers,
Chris
