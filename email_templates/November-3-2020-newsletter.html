Weekly Newsletter No. 10
Happy Wednesday as always,

Happy post-Halloween or beginning of November. Either seems appropriate. This Halloween seemed like the end of something. What, I can’t quite put my finger on. Not a bad end or a good end, just an end. Maybe the weather has finally given me belief that summer has ended and I can rejoice with Fall and enjoying our outdoor fireplace. On the other side of the patio lies my smoker and bbq who played tackle football with a good friend. I’ll allow your imagination to run wild regarding the outcome. Functional, yes. Though the two just look like they need to be taken out back. Recently, my father-in-law gifted me his Kamdo Joe grill and I couldn't be happier. I’ve always had a fondness for cooking with a wood fire and this gives me the opportunity to move forward with more of a primal cooking shift towards larger cuts of meat. So with that, let’s get to it.
Cooking
It’s soup season, and for me that means gumbo season. A duck, andouille, shrimp and collard greens gumbo was first on the list. Debuting high on the list, this one includes rich dark meat from the cured duck with an earthiness tone from the greens.
Reading
Climate change is a topic that will be percolating the headlines for the foreseeable future. The doom is easy to see though the hope is where I like to take my reads. The Verge recently profiled Microsoft and its pursuit to reduce the amount of water used to cool their data centers. Reading that a boiling liquid has the ability to draw heat from a server is fascinating and makes me want to rabbit-hole down basic science again.
Musing
My 12 year old Frye boots have finally given out and they will be missed. These Logan Cap Toe from Frye are on the watchlist along with the Iron Ranger style from Red Wings.
Listening
Lucy Dacus. This is one of those artists where I don’t understand why they're not more popular. All of her albums are coherent with songs written so that you can see the actions in your mind’s eye. Plus, all of her songs are catchy as hell or they rock. I don’t know how you can listen to Hot & Heavy and not bop or shake your head; it’s infectious.
Alright folks that will do it. Thanksgiving is coming. Be well.

Cheers,
Chris
