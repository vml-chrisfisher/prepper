Weekly Newsletter No. 14
Hello all,

Happy holidays! For those of you who celebrated last week, I hope you had an amazing Thanksgiving and enjoyed time with loved ones. This year, we merged families with Adrienne’s business partner, and I feel guilty admitting it, but it made me realize how much I took Thanksgiving, a day meant to be grateful, for granted. I know it’s easy for things to become normalized. If you’re always around something, unfortunately, it has a tendency to become nothing. And that’s not to say that Thanksgiving is nothing, but that because it always happened uninterrupted and was a day I could count on, I didn’t really appreciate the loneliness of being without it. So this year, whether you celebrated or not, I encourage you to take a few minutes and appreciate everything you can. And with that, let's get to it.
Ham Salad
Cooking
Besides a day or two of turkey sandwiches, I could forego Thanksgiving leftovers. Though add a ham, like we did this year, and I’m all in on making ham salad. I tend to eat salad on a cracker and not as a sandwich, which helps reduce the burnout.
For A Climate Solution, Look To The 1940’s
Reading
As a cook, I’ve always wanted to start a great garden. Interestingly, my overthinking of having the perfect Victory Garden has allowed me to produce a garden of nothing. One of the back of mind goals for Knife and Fish is to include gardening, along with cooking knowledge. For A Climate Solution, Look To The 1940’s from the Rodale Institute gives hope that we can all reduce our carbon footprint simply by planting a personal garden. I like the idea of everyone creating a circle of responsibility when it comes to their lifestyle. These days, a personal responsibility and grace would be welcome by all.
Eric Jordan
Listening
In my many years of web development, I have followed people with the intention of increasing my knowledge. One of the many followed was Eric Jordan, who started 2Advanced Studios, one of the most copied websites back in the day. However, one of Eric’s side hobbies was producing trance mixes. Mixes that last on average of an hour and a half. The emotional pulls and rises he creates are simply outstanding.
That’s it, folks. Be good and find a little compassion for someone today.
