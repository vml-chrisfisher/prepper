Weekly Newsletter No. 7
Hello All,

This week has been a nice one. Since I started this newsletter, I’ve been finding my routine of working from home, as well as practicing meaningful and timed breaks throughout the day. In other words, I’m in a groove. With a lot of irons in the fire, I worry that I might miss something. So, I’m happy to report that the structure of this groove gives me time to accomplish goals that are meaningful to me, while still allowing time to keep Knife and Fish going and enjoy the weather. My wife is having an outdoor girl’s night this week, which will always move things up the to-do list and add some recipes to cook. This new flow has me feeling organized and knowing that I’m doing everything I can to keep all of the balls from dropping and my mental health solid. Not a bad feeling at all. So, let's get started.
Cooking
The rain came on Sunday and brought the chill that Fall needed. Soups are on my mind and first up is Green Gumbo with Red Field Peas. Most gumbos are known for their mix of poultry, seafood and pork. Green gumbos are a bit earthier, highlighting the earth through the greens and red peas.
Listening
It felt good to attend the Kemper Gala and dance this weekend. It left me wanting to light a fire outside, grab a bottle of red wine and just enjoy groove dancing with the wife. Tearing at the Seams by Nathaniel Rateliff & The Night Seam provides the soundtrack for this thought. Upbeat just enough. Flowy. Raspy. Trust me, it’s good.
Drinking
If I haven’t been obvious enough, a fire and cocktail with the Fall air is high on my list these days. The Fall moves me into bourbons and gins as my base with the Manhattan making the top of the list. While traditionally served neat, I prefer a singular large format cube to force the sip.

That's it, be good to each other.

Best all,
Chris
