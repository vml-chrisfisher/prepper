Weekly Newsletter No. 8
Hi all,

Another happy Wednesday as always. This last weekend I had the pleasure of celebrating my parents and their 50th wedding anniversary. I can’t remind myself of anything I’ve celebrated 50 times. It leaves me in awe to imagine the ups and downs my parents have explored together and leaves me in happiness thinking of the love I get to live in my own marriage. It also gives me a sense of accomplishment for finding the slowness of life and satisfaction that I’ve found the switch thinking of getting to versus having to. Alright, let’s get to it.
Cooking
We had extended family over for dinner this last weekend. If your family has my family’s timing then you know someone will be late. Traditionally I roast vegetables at a high heat, which I’ve written about, though I decided to roast them at 250° for around an hour or so. The flavors were deeper and cleaner. The margin of error is wider with this technique which I’ll take when dealing with my family’s timeliness.
Listening
The Lumineers are slowly releasing their newest album with the first two songs from Brightside already out. My daughter and I have simply started with Brightside and then let the algorithms take over. It has been a pleasure to introduce The Lumineers to my daughter and she’s a fan.
Using
Fall has set in and those Fatwood sticks from Plow & Hearth I talked about have been everything I wanted them to be. Taking the subject hard left, I think I’m known for, the leftover storage drawer has finally frustrated enough to throw it all out. I’ve added various sizes of ZipTop containers to the cart and patiently awaiting their arrival. The old man in me is finding happiness in this purchase and is really hoping this works in my favor.
Alright, folks, that will do it. Be well and take care of each other out there.
