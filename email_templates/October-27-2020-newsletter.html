Weekly Newsletter No. 9
Happy Wednesday as always,

The wife and I took a short vacation to Florida to celebrate the 40th birthday of one of our best friends. It was a bit shocking to come back from the perfect 72° weather to the chilly 50’s we’ve been enjoying this week. This weather makes one yearn of small road trips. Losing direction on lost long roads. Stopping at struggling “whole in the wall” destinations along the way. Hoping to find the next best pie or hash. With apples in season, a trip to the town of Westin comes to mind with a stop at an orchard to help the kids pick a couple of bags of apples. Every couple or three years, Adrienne’s Mom has maintained the tradition of making apple butter. A recipe that starts with a shot of apple brandy and a fire lit in the early morning. Bushels of apples, sugar, and spices are cooked in a heavy copper kettle over an open fire at hours on end. It’s a tradition I look forward to one day inheriting. I’ve got nothing more to share than all’s good here. I hope the same is true for you as well. And with that, let’s get to it.
Cooking
This pandemic created new ways of maintaining friendships with outdoor 3 finger whiskey drinks around a fire being a steady at our house. Fall fruit galettes, sweet or savory, are coming to mind to upgrade this, with a Brown Butter Apple Galette first on the list.
Using
Between all of the galettes, tarts and different breads my measuring cups and spoons have come to my attention. Time hasn’t been friendly to them, not to say they were made for the use given to them. These metal ones from Muted Home have caught my eye. The feel and weight look right.
Listening
Speaking of traveling lost roads with the kids brings the necessity of great traveling music. Wild Roads by Ella Grace bring feeling of seeing my daughter look through the window of the car thinking through all of her dreams.
Musing
My wax finished coats are coming out these days. There’s something about a good weighted wax-coated coat and the warmth and dryness that's just right. Though like all Fall jackets comes the smell of last year's firepit smoke. It’s a no no to wash wax-coated coats. I recently found Otterwax, with it’s fabric wax and odor eliminator and excited to use on a couple of coats that some needed love.
That's it for now. Keep warm and enjoy a glass of coversation with a friend.

Be well,
Chris
