Weekly Newsletter No. 1
To say it’s been a bit and that the world has changed would both be banal observations that have allowed me to think deeply and breathe oxygen into Knife & Fish. Diving into what it means to be Creole. Contemplating what it means to be a living part of a unique and generally misunderstood heritage. Not knowing where to sit or feel in the middle of a deeply charged and divided country. It was a humbling I didn’t know I needed. Not ignorant to everyone going through their own journey I weaved mine through cooking.

Growing with a heightened exhilaration of pursuing the techniques of French cooking, Black cooking and their unique position and how it represents so much in the meld of America. It’s given me room to shed and allowed a growing unique voice and point of view. For which I am grateful and excited. Learning history, learning French techniques, Black techniques, putting in the reps to as many as possible. Making me look at Knife & Fish and see that it needed to be something else. It needed a voice, a purpose, and a vision.

So the crayons came out. Murder maps of user journeys were drawn. Coding. Writing. All moving forward including this email. It just seems natural to give you a constant stream of the influence of all of this. Interesting articles. Bits of music. Things I’m cooking. Different musings.

It has been humbling and even more grateful you have stayed along with this journey. It doesn't go unnoticed.

So let’s get to it.
Cooking
Summer is coming to an end but that also means the peak of tomato season is here. If you didn’t grow your own, do yourself a favor and swing by your local farmer’s market and grab several. My family will be having BLT’s for dinner at least once a week for the next month. Though I’ve also been increasing my tomato intake with a Tomato, Burrata, and Olive salad. A simple salad but the olive and capers give you the salty umami you want with a good fresh tomato. Breaking open the burrata is as satisfying as cutting the yolk of a sunny side egg and is the medium that brings all of the flavors together. I like to take this salad out of the refrigerator 30 minutes before I serve it. It seems to bring the flavors out more.
Reading
The Elegant, Astounding Drumming of Charlie Watts. Man, RIP Charlie Watts. My fandom of the Rolling Stones has always been one of respect, never deep diving, knowing I always should. However, I am aware of the powerhouse that the Stones were and the fact that music today is different, partially due to the Stones and partially due to Charlie’s drumming. This article captures the fact that what the Stones did, as do so many other great musicians, can’t really be described with words. So give it a read, but better yet give them a listen.
Listening
I have so many friends, even acquaintances who have a deeper breadth of music knowledge. I aspire to have the music swagger of my friend Bill Krekic. Though to my daughter, I still have it and recently we both discovered Sons of the East. A bluegrass band that reminds you of when Old Crow released Wagon Wheel. Give them play.
Using
Palm Pot Brush I’ve been using these bamboo brushes to clean everything. Regular dishes, non-stick pans with the softer brush and the stiff brush is amazing for cast iron skillets that need a little more elbow grease. I recently put these on an Amazon subscription and threw away all of the sponges and scrub brushes.
Musing
Bringing Herbs Indoors for the Winter September is here and so does the rush for Fall. Lawn bags full of leaves and the cleaning of the gardens and flower beds. Don’t throw out those herbs. Now is the time to re-pot them and acclimate them to receiving less light. I’ve jotted down some notes of things that have worked for me over the years. Keep that rosemary going and make your cast-iron steak look extra bougie this winter.
