Weekly Newsletter No. 2
Hello All,

It’s nice to pen this with a bit of quiet in the house. My nieces and nephews stayed with us over the Labor Day weekend. A houseful of 5 kids. The girls quietly playing dolls in a corner. Contrasted with the boys designing engineering marvels of orange and blue track link together with endless streams of Hot Wheels flying through. Negotiating with 5 different appetites and requests for condiments. Who knew a hot dog could be ordered in so many different ways.

The Fishers are not known for gathering frequently, with breaks known to be around a year long. Though there is something about cousins. Our kids’ eyes always light up with excitement when they find out the cousins are coming over. No matter what they seem to bond over the common goal of fun and all of the sibling fights seem to fade away. These are moments I live for. It’s an extension of why I love to cook and how it brings people together.

Last week’s rain really helped cool things down and help usher in the feelings of fall. I was finally able to verticut and reseed the lawn. Resharpen the tools, patch the outdoor fireplace, and amazingly I was able to clean out the garage so we can park both cars in the garage for this winter. Now I can concentrate on the Chief's home opener and switching the beer fridge over to IPAs.

Fall is coming and based on my loose survey most of us are ready for it. Alright, let’s do this.
Cooking
The official start of football is coming which means it’s the time of the year I go all-in on chicken wings. The distraction of the two rolls of paper towels leaves sauced wings undesired. These Crispy Salt and Pepper Wings are crispy on the outside, tender inside, and just simple. Buttermilk dressing on the side is always a welcome addition.
Reading
Hurricane Ida is just another reminder of global warming and how we are in a constant fight with Mother Nature. The Seas Are Rising. Could Oysters Help? and U.S. Civil Engineer Bent the Rules give insight into the minds of engineers and how they have to navigate the literal and political waters. It’s something that is always top of mind with me and how each day we have to keep doing small things faster.
Listening
Adrienne has always chuckled at me as I play the Killers to each of the kids for their bedtime song. Obviously, they are ballads though it also helps me cement my position as the house’s music aficionado. The Killers just released Pressure Machine and I’m a fan. The original version is a little into the craft for me so I gravitate toward the unabridged version. Parts of it reminds me of the Wallflowers original album with Jakob Dylan trying to hide his obvious influence of his father.
Musing
When Adrienne and I first met there was a bit of an old man in me that would sharpen and clean the tools for the year. Recently I’ve recentered myself toward keeping those things doing. Fall Tool Reset is a list I jotted down all of the things I’ve done throughout the years that seem to work.
Finally I have to call out my obvious mistake and the mispelling of my friend Bill Krejci's name. Billy, I apology for the mistake but never for calling you a friend and an inspiration.

Go Chiefs!!!

Best,
Chris
